Accessibility Assessment

WCAG 2.2 Success Criteria Analysis for Wellness Community Platform

Based on our client and user needs analysis, we have identified 13 key WCAG success criteria that are essential for our wellness community platform. These criteria address the specific accessibility challenges faced by our diverse user base, including elderly users with limited tech skills, individuals with visual impairments, and users requiring assistive technologies.

Perceivable - Information must be presentable to users in ways they can perceive

1.1.1

Alternative Text for Non-text Content

Why Relevant

Akasha (elderly user) relies on screen readers. Our platform images need alt text for accessibility tools to describe visual content effectively.

1.3.2

Meaningful Sequence

Why Relevant

Users navigating with keyboard/screen readers need logical content order. Essential for Akasha's screen reader and Fligo's keyboard navigation needs.

1.4.1

Avoid Color-only Cues

Why Relevant

Users with color blindness need additional indicators beyond color. Critical for form errors and interactive elements in our wellness platform.

1.4.3

Perceivable Text Contrast

Why Relevant

Akasha has vision difficulties and needs high contrast text. Minimum 4.5:1 ratio ensures readability for low vision users in our community.

1.4.4

Resize Text without Loss

Why Relevant

Akasha needs larger text sizes. Our platform must support 200% zoom without breaking layout or losing functionality.

Operable - Interface components must be operable

2.1.1

Keyboard Navigability

Why Relevant

Fligo uses keyboard navigation for privacy. All interactive elements must be accessible via Tab/Enter keys without mouse dependency.

2.4.7

Focus Visible

Why Relevant

Users navigating with keyboard need clear focus indicators. Essential for Fligo's discrete navigation and assistive technology users.

Understandable - Information and operation of interface must be understandable

3.1.5

Reading Level

Why Relevant

Akasha has limited literacy. Content must use simple language and clear explanations to ensure comprehension across education levels.

3.2.3

Consistent Navigation

Why Relevant

Akasha struggles with changing interfaces. Navigation must remain consistent across pages to reduce cognitive load and confusion.

3.2.4

Consistent Identification

Why Relevant

Interface elements must behave consistently. Critical for users like Akasha who need predictable interactions and Denisho who values reliability.

3.3.1

Error Identification & Suggestions

Why Relevant

All users need clear error messages with suggestions. Particularly important for Akasha's limited tech skills and form completion difficulties.

3.3.2

Labels & Instructions

Why Relevant

Clear form labels essential for screen readers and users with cognitive challenges. Critical for Akasha's comprehension and Fligo's efficient navigation.

3.3.4

Error Prevention and Recovery

Why Relevant

Users need confirmation for important actions. Essential for Fligo's safety concerns and Akasha's fear of making irreversible mistakes.

Implementation Suggestions

Based on my user research, these accessibility criteria directly address the needs of our wellness community users. Priority should be given to perceivable text contrast (1.4.3), keyboard navigability (2.1.1), and consistent navigation and Identification (3.2.3, 3.2.4) as these impact our most users directly.
