Ethics Analysis

Evaluating the newsletter subscription feature using ACS ethical framework

Step 1: Identify Potential Harms

Based on the user research, we identified several potential harms that could occur from implementing the newsletter subscription feature:

Akasha - Elderly User with Memory Issues

Privacy Confusion

Potential Harm: Unable to understand data collection and privacy terms

Supporting Quote: "I would need my granddaughter to check everything first... I can't read those tiny legal words anyway."

Akasha cannot independently understand privacy policies or consent forms, making her vulnerable to agreeing to data practices she doesn't comprehend.

Technology Overwhelm

Potential Harm: Complex signup processes causing exclusion and frustration

Supporting Quote: "Sometimes I touch the wrong thing and everything disappears, and then I don't know how to get back."

Complex newsletter signup processes could prevent Akasha from accessing community resources or cause distress when technology doesn't work as expected.

Family Dependency Issues

Potential Harm: Increased burden on family members and loss of autonomy

Supporting Quote: "She's very patient with me, but I feel bad always asking for help."

Newsletter management requiring technical skills could increase Akasha's dependence on her granddaughter, reducing her sense of independence.

Denisho - Professional Facing Workplace Discrimination

Privacy Risks

Potential Harm: Workplace monitoring revealing personal information

Supporting Quote: "I'm careful about what I search for - I don't want my IT department or supervisors seeing that I'm looking up things about workplace discrimination or family counseling."

Newsletter subscriptions related to family support or discrimination could be discovered by workplace IT, potentially affecting Denisho's employment.

Data Security Fears

Potential Harm: Inadequate protection of sensitive personal information

Supporting Quote: "I'd want to understand exactly how the platform protects user data. Is it encrypted? Who has access? How long is information stored?"

Denisho's financial background makes him particularly aware of data security risks. Poor newsletter data protection could expose his family situation.

Identity Exposure Risks

Potential Harm: Multicultural family situation being exposed inappropriately

Supporting Quote: "My son doesn't need additional scrutiny at school, my wife doesn't need workplace complications, and I certainly can't afford to give my supervisors any more ammunition against me."

Newsletter data collection could inadvertently reveal information about Denisho's multicultural family, affecting his son's school experience and his own workplace situation.

Fligo - Transgender Teacher

Safety Through Anonymity Risks

Potential Harm: Family monitoring discovering transgender identity

Supporting Quote: "My family still checks my devices sometimes, which is invasive but that's the reality I'm dealing with right now."

Newsletter subscriptions to LGBTQ+ resources could be discovered by unsupportive family members, potentially leading to rejection or harm.

Professional Exposure

Potential Harm: Teaching career jeopardized by gender identity exposure

Supporting Quote: "Teaching can be... complicated when you're trans, especially in more conservative areas."

Newsletter data linking to Fligo's work email or being discoverable through professional networks could threaten her teaching position.

Identity Protection Concerns

Potential Harm: Deadnaming or identity invalidation through poor data practices

Supporting Quote: "The ability to use chosen names and pronouns consistently throughout the platform is non-negotiable. And I'd need assurance that my legal name would never be visible."

Newsletter systems that require legal names or fail to respect chosen names could cause psychological harm and identity invalidation.

Step 1 Conclusion

Our analysis reveals that the newsletter subscription feature poses significant ethical challenges across different user groups. The harms identified range from privacy violations and technology exclusion to professional risks and identity safety concerns. These findings demonstrate why a careful ethical framework is essential before implementation.

Step 2: ACS Values & Principles → Solutions

For each identified harm, we apply relevant ACS values and principles to develop ethical solutions:

Privacy Protection

ACS Value: Honesty & Respect

Principle: "Minimize harm" and "Respect others' views"

Solutions

• Clear, simple privacy policies
• Optional data collection only
• Easy opt-out mechanisms
• Family-friendly consent processes

Digital Accessibility

ACS Value: Respect

Purpose: "Enhancement of quality of life"

Solutions

• Large text options
• Simple signup processes
• Voice-based alternatives
• Family assistance features

Professional Safety

ACS Value: Trustworthiness

Purpose: "Primacy of public interest"

Solutions

• Anonymous subscription options
• Encrypted data storage
• No workplace email requirements
• Secure browsing features

Identity Respect

ACS Value: Respect & Honesty

Principle: "Take into account others' points of view"

Solutions

• Chosen name support
• No legal name requirements
• Inclusive language design
• Identity validation features

Data Security

ACS Value: Trustworthiness

Principle: "Minimize harm"

Solutions

• End-to-end encryption

• User data control options

Community Trust

ACS Value: Honesty & Respect

Purpose: "Enhancement of quality of life"

Solutions

Step 3: Managing Tensions

Implementing ethical newsletter practices creates tensions between client expectations and user protection needs:

Client vs. User Privacy

Tension: Client wants comprehensive user data for marketing effectiveness

Our Approach: Prioritize minimal data collection while demonstrating how trust-building leads to higher long-term engagement than aggressive data harvesting.

Options for Client: Offer analytics on engagement patterns without collecting personal data, focus on content quality over user profiling.

Functionality vs. Accessibility

Tension: Advanced features may exclude users with different abilities or tech skills

Our Approach: Design with progressive enhancement - basic functionality accessible to all, advanced features as opt-in enhancements.

Options for Client: Multi-tier approach with simple and advanced interfaces, family assistance features, voice alternatives.

Efficiency vs. Consent

Tension: Detailed consent processes may reduce subscription rates

Our Approach: Design intuitive consent that respects autonomy without creating barriers. Clear value proposition for data sharing.

Options for Client: Gradual consent model, clear benefits explanation, community-first messaging over data-first messaging.

Recommended Ethical Approach

Based on ACS principles and our user research, we recommend implementing a privacy-first newsletter system that prioritizes user safety, accessibility, and autonomy over data collection efficiency. This approach aligns with the "primacy of public interest" and "enhancement of quality of life" purposes while building sustainable community trust.

The ethical framework demonstrates that protecting our most vulnerable users (elderly, discriminated workers, marginalized identities) ultimately creates a safer, more inclusive platform for all community members.
