Work Phase 3: Design Proposal

A comprehensive style guide demonstrating design decisions across visceral, behavioural, and reflective levels for a community wellness platform.

About This Proposal

This design proposal presents a complete style guide for a community platform supporting the Connecting Communities Initiative (CCI). The design responds to extensive user research and applies Norman's three levels of design—visceral, behavioural, and reflective—to create an accessible, inclusive, and meaningful digital experience.

The style guide documents design decisions through practical demonstrations of visual systems, interactive components, and values-driven design choices. Each section includes rationale grounded in user needs, accessibility standards, and ethical considerations.

Design Approach

This proposal demonstrates user-centered design methodology, beginning with research-based understanding of community needs and concluding with concrete implementation decisions. The approach prioritizes accessibility, privacy, and cultural sensitivity while maintaining visual appeal and functional effectiveness.

Design decisions are documented with clear rationale connecting user research findings to specific implementation choices. Accessibility considerations are integrated throughout rather than treated as compliance checkboxes. Ethical implications are examined openly, acknowledging tensions between organizational goals and user protection.

Core Design Principles

Research-Grounded Design

Every design decision traces back to user research findings. Visual choices, interaction patterns, and content strategies respond to documented user needs rather than designer preferences or aesthetic trends. This ensures the platform serves actual community members rather than idealized personas.

Accessibility as Foundation

Accessibility considerations inform design from the beginning rather than being added retroactively. WCAG 2.2 Level AA compliance is baseline; design goes beyond minimum standards to support users with diverse abilities, technology comfort levels, and situational constraints.

Privacy as Safety

Privacy features protect users facing discrimination, surveillance, or vulnerability. Design choices prioritize user safety over data collection convenience, recognizing that trust-building serves long-term organizational goals better than aggressive analytics.

Inclusive Visual Language

Visual and verbal design avoids stereotypes, represents diverse experiences, and respects how people describe themselves. Language follows inclusive terminology standards; imagery shows genuine interaction rather than stock photo aesthetics.

Proposal Structure

This design proposal demonstrates competency across technical implementation, accessibility standards, ethical reasoning, and critical thinking. The style guide provides practical documentation suitable for developer handoff while explaining design rationale for client understanding.
